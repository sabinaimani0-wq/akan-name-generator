About the Akan Namings System

Akan naming traditions is a Ghanan naming tadition that involves giving a child (kradin) a name based on the day of the week they are born, which is believed to influence their personality. An "outdooring" ceremony (abadinto) takes place eight days after birth to formally welcome the child into the community and introduce their formal or ancestral name.Akan people believe the day of birth determines the child's kra (soul) and influences their personality.The child is formally introduced to the community and ancestors on the eighth day after birth. The eight-day waiting period is meant to ensure the baby survives the first week, moving from the spirit world to the physical world. A godparent lifts the child three times. The child is told to respect ancestors and elders.
